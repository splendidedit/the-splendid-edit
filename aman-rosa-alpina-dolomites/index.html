Aman opens the alpine year above San Cassiano

By The Splendid Edit May 2026 5 min read
← Back to The Edit

A short drive north of Venice, the road climbs into a UNESCO valley and the air thins. This is where Aman has set its first Italian mountain resort, in time for the summer trails.

San Cassiano is small, tucked into the Alta Badia at the foot of the Dolomites. The pale walls of the peaks come up close, then fall back as the road turns. Rosa Alpina has stood here since 1850. Aman took it on, closed it for a long renovation, and opened the new resort under its own name. The summer season runs from June through October.

The house

There are 51 rooms and suites now, with Jean-Michel Gathy on the design. The interiors keep to mountain materials, pale wood, soft stone, low light. Most rooms face either the village or the forest, with the peaks framed by the windows. Premier Suites carry their own fireplaces and balconies, and the Rosa Alpina Suite sits at the top floor with views that run the length of the valley.

The building stays close to its Alpine roots. The pitch of the roofs, the simple railings, the cuts of the windows all read as San Cassiano rather than imported Aman. Inside, the lines lengthen, the palette quietens. The brand's mark is felt more than seen.

The outdoor pool at Aman Rosa Alpina · Courtesy of Aman

The spa

Aman Spa runs across two floors. Seven treatment rooms, three pools, a hammam, sauna and steam, and a long indoor lap pool lit by warm wood. The outdoor pool sits above the village, level with the tree line. Treatments draw on the mountain, with stone and pine and arnica turning up in the menus. Day rituals are built for guests stepping in after the trails or off the lifts.

This is the part of an Aman that most visibly carries the brand. The light is held low, the rooms are wide and quiet, the corridors lead in slow turns. A wellness escape package layers consultations, treatments and movement across a stay. There is no rush.

Beyond the spa, the resort holds five restaurants and a lounge. The Grill works the terrace through summer with Italian cooking pitched at the family table. Enju is the Japanese counter, built around shabu shabu and the kind of slow dining that suits a long alpine evening. Il Salotto is the lounge bar. Breakfast moves into the Heritage Room, with the forest at the window. The Di Vino Room handles cellar dinners.

The mountain

San Cassiano sits inside Dolomiti Superski, the largest linked ski network in the world, with more than 1,200 kilometres of pistes across a dozen valleys. Winter is the headline. The summer is the secret. Between June and October, the lifts run for walkers and bikers, the via ferrata routes open across the cliffs, and the meadows turn deep green.

Aman builds the warm-weather programme around the landscape. Sunrise hikes leave before dawn for the ridge above the resort. Skyrunning trails climb hard and fast through the higher passes. Mountain biking moves between forest roads and singletrack. For slower days there is yoga on the deck, a long lunch on the Grill terrace, the cold pool, then the sauna.

Sunrise on the ridge above San Cassiano · Courtesy of Aman

Getting in

Venice Marco Polo is the easy arrival, two and a half hours by car through the foothills and into the valley. Bolzano cuts it to ninety minutes. Innsbruck from the north sits at around two. The brand also runs helicopter transfers when the booking calls for it, forty-five minutes from Venice, twenty-two from Bolzano, around an hour from Milan.

The summer pattern at an Italian Alpine resort tends toward a long week, two if the weather behaves. Hike one day, ride the next, take a slow Sunday between them. The valley is wide enough to keep the days different from each other.

The Dolomites are at their best when the snow has gone and the high meadows have come in. Aman Rosa Alpina is open for them now, and through the autumn. It is the brand's first proper mountain property in Italy, and the most natural fit it has had with a Western landscape in a long time.

Aman Rosa Alpina is open from June through October for the summer season, and from December into spring for the ski months. Rates and bookings through aman.com.
