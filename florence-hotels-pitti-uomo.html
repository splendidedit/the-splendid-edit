The Stay — Florence · Pitti Uomo June 2026

Where to stay in Florence during Pitti Uomo.

The Splendid Edit Florence · June 2026 8 min read
← Back to The Edit

Pitti Uomo takes Florence in a particular way. The city does not transform itself for the week — it simply allows it, with the serene confidence of somewhere that has absorbed considerably more dramatic events. What changes is the rate. And the availability. And the quality of service at the hotels that know the industry has arrived.

Booking for Pitti Uomo’s June edition — which this year features Simone Rocha as guest designer — should happen now. The reliable addresses fill before April. What follows is The Splendid Edit’s assessment of where to base operations, organised not by price but by function: what each hotel does best for the specific rhythms of the trade fair week.

Four Seasons Firenze — the operational choice

The Four Seasons Firenze occupies a fifteenth-century palazzo on the Borgo Pinti, with eleven acres of private garden — the largest privately held garden in Florence. During Pitti week, this garden performs a function that no other hotel in the city can replicate: it provides genuine decompression from a schedule that has no natural pause.

The rooms and suites occupy both the historic palazzo and a converted convent, with the distinction being significant: the convent suites have a stillness and a height that the palazzo rooms, beautiful as they are, do not quite match. The service operates at the level one expects from the brand, which at this property means it operates at the level one expects from Florence at its best.

The Pitti Uomo shuttle runs from Borgo Pinti to the Fortezza in under ten minutes. The spa provides the only legitimate argument for leaving the garden. The breakfast is the kind that makes arriving at the first show on time genuinely difficult.

Portrait Firenze — the location argument

Portrait Firenze’s position on the Lungarno Acciaiuoli — directly on the Arno, forty metres from the Ponte Vecchio — is the kind of address that makes every decision about where to eat and where to walk resolve itself without effort. The hotel occupies a sixteenth-century palazzo and operates with the scale of a private house: forty rooms and suites, service that is attentive in the specific way that small numbers permit.

The rooftop terrace, which takes in the river, the bridges, and the hills beyond, is one of the city’s better arguments for arriving before the week begins rather than on opening day. The Lungarno Collection’s relationship with Salvatore Ferragamo — the family that owns the hotel — means the fashion industry is a constituency the property understands without making a point of it.

For Pitti week specifically, the location places the Fortezza da Basso at approximately twenty minutes on foot, which is either an advantage or a commute depending on one’s constitution and the June heat.

Hotel Savoy — the Piazza della Repubblica address

The Hotel Savoy sits on the Piazza della Repubblica, which is either the city’s most convenient central address or its most obvious one, depending on one’s tolerance for a square that exists primarily for tourists and the industry professionals who have temporarily become them. The Rocco Forte property has managed the tension well. The rooms are genuinely good — the renovation completed several years ago introduced a quality of material and a seriousness of proportion that the old Savoy had lost.

The Irene restaurant on the ground floor operates at a level that justifies eating in rather than navigating the week’s restaurant reservations. The bar, which opens onto the piazza, is where the industry actually goes in the evenings once the dinners have finished and a certain looseness of schedule becomes possible.

One address that does not justify the rate

Florence during Pitti week produces a reliable category of hotel: the property that has understood that the week generates demand without understanding that demand requires justification. The rates go up. The service does not. The rooms, which are perfectly adequate during the quieter months, begin to feel like they are being asked to perform above their ability.

The Splendid Edit’s position on this is consistent: a hotel does not earn the right to a Pitti rate simply by being in Florence in June. It earns it by understanding what the industry guest actually needs — reliable WiFi at hours that do not conform to Italian mealtimes, a car service that grasps the concept of punctuality, a concierge who knows that “sold out” is not an answer that the week accepts.

The addresses above meet those requirements. Several prominent properties in the historic centre do not, and their names are known to the industry through the accumulated experience of several seasons. Book accordingly.
