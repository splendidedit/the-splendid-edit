Where to stay in Florence during Pitti Uomo

By Juliette Marchand Florence · June 2026 8 min read
← Back to The Edit

Pitti Uomo takes Florence in a particular way. The city does not transform itself for the week; it simply allows it, with the serene confidence of somewhere that has absorbed more dramatic events. What changes is the rate, the availability, the quality of service at hotels that know the industry has arrived.

Book now for June. Simone Rocha is guest designer this year. The reliable addresses fill before April. What follows is an assessment of where to base operations, organized by function: what each hotel does best for the specific rhythms of the trade fair week.

Four Seasons Firenze / The operational choice

Fifteen-century palazzo on the Borgo Pinti. Eleven acres of private garden. The largest privately held garden in Florence. During Pitti week, this garden does what no other hotel in the city can: it provides genuine breathing room from a schedule that never stops.

Rooms and suites occupy both the historic palazzo and a converted convent. The convent suites have a stillness and height the palazzo rooms cannot match. Service here means what service means in Florence at its best.

The Pitti Uomo shuttle reaches the Fortezza in under ten minutes. The spa gives real reason to leave the garden. Breakfast arrives early and lingers.

Portrait Firenze / The location

Lungarno Acciaiuoli, directly on the Arno, forty metres from the Ponte Vecchio. This address decides everything: where to eat, where to walk. Sixteenth-century palazzo. Forty rooms and suites. Small enough for attention to matter.

The rooftop terrace takes in the river, the bridges, the hills beyond. One argument alone for arriving before opening day. The Lungarno Collection owns this through the Ferragamo family. Fashion industry means something different here.

Twenty minutes on foot to the Fortezza da Basso. Either advantage or commute depending on the June heat.

Florence in June

Hotel Savoy / Piazza della Repubblica

Piazza della Repubblica. Convenient. Obvious. Rocco Forte has managed this tension well. The renovation introduced material and proportion the old Savoy had lost.

Irene restaurant on the ground floor justifies staying in. The bar opens onto the piazza. Where the industry goes in the evenings when dinners finish and schedules loosen.

The hotels that don't justify the rate

Pitti week produces a reliable category of hotel: properties that understand the week generates demand but not that demand requires justification. Rates go up. Service does not. Rooms adequate in quieter months feel stretched.

A hotel does not earn the right to premium pricing simply by being in Florence in June. It earns it by understanding what the industry guest needs. Reliable WiFi at hours that don't conform to Italian mealtimes. A car service that grasps punctuality. A concierge who knows that "sold out" is not acceptable.

The hotels above meet these requirements. Several prominent properties in the historic centre do not. Their names are known through accumulated experience. Book accordingly.
