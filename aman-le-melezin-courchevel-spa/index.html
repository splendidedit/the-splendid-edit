Aman raises the hotel to make room for the spa

By The Splendid Edit May 2026 5 min read
← Back to The Edit

A four-storey chalet on the Bellecôte Piste was lifted on stilts for a summer. Engineers built a spa underneath. The hotel comes back on 10 December.

Aman Le Mélézin opened in 1992, the brand's only French address, and for thirty-four winters it sat directly on the snow at Courchevel 1850. The site is small, the village dense, the ski-in ski-out line non-negotiable. To grow the spa, there was nowhere to go but down.

Over the summer of 2026, the building was raised on steel jacks and held in mid-air while crews excavated and built two new floors beneath it. The technique is common in tight urban sites in Paris and Tokyo. In Courchevel, where the season is short and the soil is alpine, it had not been tried before.

The lift

The rebuild adds 767 square metres of spa across two floors, all of it under the hotel that already stood there. The new programme runs alongside the existing pool, hammam, sauna and gym, which carry over from the previous spa. Five large treatment rooms come in, including two for couples and a dedicated Thai massage suite.

Christophe Tollemer ran the interiors, working with Aman's in-house design team. Tollemer has been one of the brand's quieter collaborators for years, sitting behind a number of the European properties. His Courchevel work leans on warm timber, low light and stone, the kind of materials that hold the heat after a long day in cold air.

The pool at Aman Le Mélézin, Courchevel · Courtesy of Aman

The hotel

The hotel itself is the same four storeys it has always been, on Rue de Bellecôte, with the piste in front of it. Aman counts thirty-one keys here, from Chambres up through the Vanoise Suite and the larger Mélézin Suite, several with private balconies and a few with outdoor jacuzzis cut into the terrace. Most look directly onto the slope.

The dining side has also moved. Yakiniku on the Terrace is the new venue, a Japanese grill set on the outdoor deck. Premium cuts arrive raw and are cooked in front of the table over glowing embers. The Salon stays, the bar stays, the Japanese tea ceremony stays. Nama, the brand's signature Japanese restaurant, returns to the building.

The Splendid Edit

Les Trois Vallées is the largest linked ski area in the world, six hundred kilometres of piste, and the Bellecôte runs straight past the door. Courchevel 1850 is the highest of the three Courchevel villages, the one with the smaller crowd and the older crowd. Aman Le Mélézin is one of three Aman ski addresses now, alongside Aman Rosa Alpina in the Italian Dolomites and Aman Niseko on Hokkaido, still to come.

Geneva is a two-and-a-half-hour drive. Lyon Saint-Exupéry runs the same. The brand has been adding ski properties faster than usual; the spa here is the visible part of a longer project to keep Le Mélézin in step with what an Aman skier now expects on the European side.

December bookings have been quietly held since the announcement landed in November. The brand has not put a number on the spa rebuild, and it is unlikely to. The point of the project is the line outside: a snow road, then a door, then a longer afternoon than there used to be.

Aman Le Mélézin reopens for the winter season on 10 December 2026. Reservations through aman.com.
